The Bakery

Established shortly after the release of X&Y, primarily as an comfort studio to escape the tumultuous chaos of that era, The Bakery has been Coldplay's home studio ever since. Built inside an old bakery (hence the name), and nestled in between a restaurant and a real estate agents' on Fleet Road, Hampstead, the Bakery will more often than not have a few diehard fans hanging around, hoping for a glimpse of one of the members.

The Bakery served as the primary location for the development and recording of Viva, decorated with massive banners reading "The King is Dead / Long Live the King" and other such revolutionary fare during the time period.

Church in Barcelona

Unfortunately, not much is known about the recordings done outside the Bakery. All we have is one solitary clip that depicts the band singing Lost! together in some old church that is allegedly in Barcelona. Perhaps one day we will unearth more details.
